Research Experiences for Undergraduates (REU) for EAGER: Crowd-AI Sensing Based Traffic Analysis for HCMC Planning Simulation

Tam Nguyen (Principal Investigator) tnguyen1@udayton.edu

News Article Department of State Highlights

Summary

  • This REU Supplement program supports two (2) University of Dayton undergraduate students during one academic year. The main goal is to provide undergraduate students with an in-depth, hands-on research experience focused on artificial intelligence (AI), object modeling and urban simulation. The students will be mentored and will assist with various research tasks, e.g., data collection and annotation, AI model training, 3D modeling, and simulation development. The recruited students must be US citizens or Permanent residents.
  • In particular, this program seeks to engage undergraduate students interested in the fields of computer science or engineering who wish to pursue intensive research opportunities in a supportive and engaging learning environment. The emphasis will be on computer vision and 3D simulation, areas that have extensive practical applications from education, entertainment to healthcare, and beyond. Below are the research activities, with the term “research” used broadly to include activities that produce new knowledge, new interpretations of information, or new expressions of creativity.
    • 1. Enhance student knowledge in the field of computer science and artificial intelligence
    • 2. Increase student interest and engagement by integrating object modeling into their research activities
    • 3. Enhance student scientific writing, communication, and collaborative skills
    • 4. Improve students’ ability to apply algorithmic principles and computing theory to applications involving 3D modeling, computer vision, and simulation.
    • 5. Broaden participation in research among underrepresented students

  • The REU call is here. Please email your CV to Prof. Tam Nguyen (tnguyen1@udayton.edu) if you are interested in joining this REU program.

Research Activities

Through this program, the students will gain hands-on experience with state-of-the-art tools and models. For example, the students will collect video frames and annotate each frame with a certain class label. They will then use the collected and annotated data to train AI models such as YOLOv5, Faster RCNN and DeepSORT. Regarding the object modeling, the students will use Blender and Maya for 3D modeling, rigging, and animation. For the simulator development, the students will learn how to embed the designed 3D models into a simulator within Unity 3D game engine. In addition to the hands-on experience, participating in this REU program is a great confidence builder for the students and helps them gain new insight into the research field.
